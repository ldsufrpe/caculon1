In this case, we can just differentiate our equation by \(t\) to get

\begin{align*} \dfrac{\dd V}{\dd t} \amp= 4 \pi r^2 \dfrac{\dd r}{\dd t}\\ \end{align*}

This can then be rearranged to give

\begin{align*} \dfrac{\dd r}{\dd t} \amp= \frac{1}{4\pi r^2} \dfrac{\dd V}{\dd t}. \end{align*}

Now we were told that \(\dfrac{\dd V}{\dd t} = 13\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} \dfrac{\dd r}{\dd t} \amp= \frac{13}{4\pi r^2}. \end{align*}

We were also told that the radius is \(15cm\text{,}\) so at that moment in time

\begin{align*} \dfrac{\dd r}{\dd t} \amp= \frac{13}{\pi 4 \times 15^2}. \end{align*}
