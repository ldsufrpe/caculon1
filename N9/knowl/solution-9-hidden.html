Derivando implicitamente em relação a \(x\) produz

\begin{equation*} \frac{9}{2 \sqrt{9x+8y}} + \frac{8}{2 \sqrt{9x+8y}} \, \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{2}{2 \sqrt{2xy}} \left( y + x \, \frac{dy}{dx} \right) = 0, \end{equation*}

ou (multiplicando por \(2 \sqrt{2xy} \sqrt{9x+8y}\))

\begin{equation*} 9 \sqrt{2xy} + 8 \sqrt{2xy} \; \frac{dy}{dx} + 2 y \sqrt{9x+8y} + 2 x \sqrt{9x+8y} \; \frac{dy}{dx} = 0, \end{equation*}

Assim

\begin{equation*} \left( 8 \sqrt{2xy} + 2 x \sqrt{9x+8y} \right) \, \frac{dy}{dx} = - \left( 9 \sqrt{2xy} + 2 y \sqrt{9x+8y} \right), \end{equation*}

e portanto

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = - \frac {9 \sqrt{2xy} + 2 y \sqrt{9x+8y}}{8 \sqrt{2xy} + 2 x \sqrt{9x+8y}}. \end{equation*}

Se \(x = 4\) e \(y = 8\text{,}\) então \(\sqrt{9x+8y} = 10\text{,}\) \(\sqrt{2xy} = 8\text{,}\) e portanto a inclinação \(m\) da reta tangente à curva no ponto \((4, 8)\) é

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} \Bigg|_{(x, y) = (4, 8)} = - \frac {9 \left( 8 \right) + 2 \left( 8 \right) \left( 10 \right)} {8 \left( 8 \right) + 2 \left( 4 \right) \left( 10 \right)} = -{\textstyle\frac{29}{18}}. \end{equation*}
