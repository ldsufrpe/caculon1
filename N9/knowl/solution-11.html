Solução 1.6.8.1.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} \dfrac{d}{dx}[7 \sin x + 3 \cos y] \amp =\amp \dfrac{d}{dx}[2]\\ \amp \amp \\ 7 \cos x - 3 \sin y \dfrac{dy}{dx} \amp =\amp 0 \\ \amp \amp \\ \dfrac{dy}{dx} \amp =\amp \dfrac{7 \cos x}{3 \sin y} \end{array} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} \dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2} \amp =\amp \dfrac{d}{dx}\left[ \dfrac{7 \cos x}{3 \sin y} \right] \\ \amp \amp \\ \amp =\amp \dfrac{ 3 \sin y(-7 \sin x) - (7 \cos x)(3 \cos y \cdot y')} {9 \sin^2 y} \\ \amp \amp \\ \amp =\amp \dfrac{-21( \sin x \sin y + \cos x \cos y \cdot y')} {9 \sin^2 y} \\ \amp =\amp \dfrac{-7( \sin x \sin y + \cos x \cos y \cdot y')} {3 \sin^2 y} \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}

Para a resposta final, substitua \(y' = \dfrac{7 \cos x}{3 \sin y}\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} \amp =\amp \dfrac{-7\left( \sin x \sin y + \cos x \cos y \cdot \dfrac{7 \cos x}{3 \sin y}\right)} {3 \sin^2 y}\\ \amp \amp \\ \amp =\amp \dfrac{-7\left( \sin x \sin y + \frac{7}{3} \cos^2 x \cot y\right)} {3 \sin^2 y}\\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
