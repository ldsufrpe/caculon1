Use as regras \(\frac{d}{dx}[ \sin x ] = \cos x\) e \(\frac{d}{dx}[ \cos x ] = -\sin x\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} g(x) \amp =\amp -6 \cos x \\ \amp \amp \\ g'(x) \amp =\amp + 6 \sin x \\ \amp \amp \\ g''(x) \amp =\amp + 6 \cos x \\ \amp \amp \\ g'''(x) \amp =\amp -6 \sin x \\ \amp \amp \\ g^{(4)}(x) \amp =\amp -6 \cos x \\ \amp \amp \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
