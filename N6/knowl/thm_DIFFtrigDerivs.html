Teorema 1.27. Derivatives of trigonometric functions.

The derivatives of \(\sin x\) and \(\cos x\) are

\begin{align*} \frac{\dd}{\dd x} \sin x \amp= \cos x \amp \frac{\dd}{\dd x} \cos x \amp= - \sin x \end{align*}

Consequently the derivatives of the other trigonometric functions are

\begin{align*} \frac{\dd}{\dd x} \tan x \amp= \sec^2 x \amp \frac{\dd}{\dd x} \cot x \amp= -\csc^2 x\\ \frac{\dd}{\dd x} \csc x \amp= -\csc x \cot x \amp \frac{\dd}{\dd x} \sec x \amp= \sec x \tan x \end{align*}
