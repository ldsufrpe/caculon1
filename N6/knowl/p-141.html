Parágrafo

\begin{align*} \sin(a+b) \amp= \sin(a) \cos(b) + \cos(a) \sin(b)\\ \cos(a+b) \amp= \cos(a) \cos(b) - \sin(a) \sin(b) \end{align*}

Para calcular a derivada de \(\sin(x)\) começamos com a definição da derivada:

\begin{align*} \frac{\dd}{\dd x}\sin x \amp=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\sin (x+h)-\sin x}{h}\\ \amp=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\sin x\cos h +\cos x\sin h-\sin x}{h}\\ \amp=\lim_{h\to 0}\bigg[\sin x\frac{\cos h-1}{h} +\cos x\frac{\sin h-0}{h}\bigg]\\ \amp=\sin x\left( \lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\cos h-1}{h}\right) +\left( \cos x\ \lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\sin h}{h}\right) \amp \ct{Aplique o }\, \knowl{./knowl/lema-limit-trig.html}{\text{Lema 1.32}}\\ \amp=\sin x\cdot 0 + \cos x\cdot 1 \\ \amp=\cos x \end{align*}

O cálculo da derivada de \(\cos x\) é muito semelhante.

\begin{align*} \frac{\dd}{\dd x}\cos x \amp=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\cos (x+h)-\cos x}{h}\\ \amp=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\cos x\cos h -\sin x\sin h-\cos x}{h}\\ \amp=\lim_{h\to 0}\bigg[\cos x\frac{\cos h-1}{h} -\sin x\frac{\sin h-0}{h}\bigg]\\ \amp=\cos x\ \lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\cos h-1}{h} \ -\ \sin x\ \lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\sin h-0}{h}\\ \amp=\cos x\left( \lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\cos h-1}{h}\right) - \sin x\left( \lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\sin h}{h}\right) \amp \ct{Aplique o }\, \knowl{./knowl/lema-limit-trig.html}{\text{Lema 1.32}}\\ \amp=\cos x\cdot 0 - \sin x\cdot 1 \\ \amp=-\sin{x} \end{align*}

Agora encontramos as derivadas de \(\sin x\) e \(\cos x\text{,}\) desde que \(x\) seja medido em radianos.
