Parágrafo

Agora é fácil obter as derivadas do restante funções trigonométricas usando identidades trigonométricas básicas e o regra do quociente. Lembrar  1  aquele

\begin{align*} \tan x\amp= \frac{\sin x}{\cos x} \amp \cot x \amp= \frac{\cos x}{\sin x}= \frac{1}{\tan x}\\ \csc x\amp= \frac{1}{\sin x} \amp \sec x \amp= \frac{1}{\cos x} \end{align*}

Então, pela regra do quociente,

\begin{align*} \frac{\dd}{\dd x}[\tan x] \amp=\frac{\dd}{\dd x}\,\left[\frac{\sin x}{\cos x} \right]\amp \amp=\frac{\overbrace{\big({\frac{\dd}{\dd x}}\sin x\big)}^{\cos x}\cos x -\sin x\overbrace{\big({\frac{\dd}{\dd x}\cos x}\big)}^{-\sin x}} {\cos^2 x} \amp \amp=\sec^2 x\\ \frac{\dd}{\dd x}\,[\csc x] \amp=\frac{\dd}{\dd x}\,\left[\frac{1}{\sin x}\right] \amp \amp=-\frac{\overbrace{\big({\frac{\dd}{\dd x}}\sin x\big)}^{\cos x}} {\sin^2 x} \amp \amp=-\csc x\cot x\\ \frac{\dd}{\dd x}\,[\sec x] \amp=\frac{\dd}{\dd x}\,\left[\frac{1}{\cos x} \right]\amp \amp=-\frac{\overbrace{\big({\frac{\dd}{\dd x}}\cos x\big)}^{-\sin x}} {\cos^2 x} \amp \amp=\sec x\tan x\\ \frac{\dd}{\dd x}\cot x \amp=\frac{\dd}{\dd x}\,\left[\frac{\cos x}{\sin x} \right]\amp \amp=\frac{ \overbrace{\big({\frac{\dd}{\dd x}}\cos x\big)}^{-\sin x}\sin x -\cos x\overbrace{{\big(\frac{\dd}{\dd x}}\sin x\big)}^{\cos x}} {\sin^2 x} \amp \amp=-\csc^2x \end{align*}
