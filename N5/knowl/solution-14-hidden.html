Solução.

Usando a definição de limite da derivada para \(\small{f(x) = \sqrt{5x+9}}\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{align} \small{f\;'(x)} \amp = \small{\displaystyle \lim_{h\to 0}} \frac{\sqrt{5\!\left(x+h\right)+9}-\sqrt{5x+9}}{h} \\ \amp = \small{\displaystyle \lim_{h\to 0}} \left(\frac{\sqrt{5\!\left(x+h\right)+9}-\sqrt{5x+9}}{h}\right) \left(\frac{\sqrt{5\!\left(x+h\right)+9}+\sqrt{5x+9}}{\sqrt{5\!\left(x+h\right)+9}+\sqrt{5x+9}}\right) \\ \amp = \small{\displaystyle \lim_{h\to 0}} \frac{5\!\left(x+h\right)+9-5x-9}{h\!\left(\sqrt{5\!\left(x+h\right)+9}+\sqrt{5x+9}\right)} \\ \amp = \small{\displaystyle \lim_{h\to 0}} \frac{5}{\sqrt{5\!\left(x+h\right)+9}+\sqrt{5x+9}} \\ \amp = \frac{5}{2\sqrt{5x+9}} \\ \end{align} \end{equation*}

Usando isso, a inclinação da linha tangente em\(\small{x = -1}\) é \(\small{f'(-1) = \normalsize{\frac{5}{2\sqrt{5(-1) + 9}} = {\textstyle\frac{5}{4}}}}\text{.}\)

\(\small{f(-1) = 2}\text{,}\) então encontramos a equação da reta tangente no ponto \(\small{(-1,2)}\) e,

\begin{equation*} \small{y - 2 =} \normalsize{{\textstyle\frac{5}{4}}} \small{(x + 1)}, \end{equation*}

ou

\begin{equation*} \normalsize{y = {\textstyle\frac{5}{4}}x+{\textstyle\frac{13}{4}}}. \end{equation*}
