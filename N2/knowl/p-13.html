Seja m \(a,c \) números reais, \(f(x)\) e \(g(x)\) funções em que o limite existe quando \(x\) tente para \(a\text{.}\) Então,

  1. \(\displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to a} ( f(x) + g(x) ) = \lim\limits_{x \to a} f(x) + \lim\limits_{x \to a}g(x) \)

  2. \(\displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to a} ( f(x) - g(x) ) = \lim\limits_{x \to a} f(x) - \lim\limits_{x \to a}g(x) \)

  3. \(\lim\limits_{x \to a} c f(x) = c \lim\limits_{x \to a} c f(x)\text{.}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to a}f(x)g(x)= \lim\limits_{x \to a} f(x) \cdot \lim\limits_{x \to a}g(x)\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to a}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}= \frac{\lim\limits_{x \to a} f(x)}{\lim\limits_{x \to a}g(x)} \quad\text{se } \lim\limits_{x \to a}g(x)\neq 0\)
