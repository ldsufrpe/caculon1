Compute the following limit:

\begin{gather*} \lim\limits_{h \to 0} \frac{(1+h)^2-1}{h} \end{gather*}

Numerator and denominator both go to zero as \(h\to 0\text{.}\) So factor and simplify:

\begin{align*} \lim\limits_{h \to 0} \frac{(1+h)^2-1}{h} \amp= \lim\limits_{h \to 0} \frac{1+2h+h^2-1}{h}\\ \amp= \lim\limits_{h \to 0} 2+h = 2 \end{align*}
