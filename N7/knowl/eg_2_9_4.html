Exemplo 1.12. Encontrando a regra do quociente da Regra da Cadeia.

We can also use the chain rule to recover CorollaryÂ [cross-reference to target(s) "cor_diff_recip" missing or not unique] and from there we can use the product rule to recover the quotient rule.

We want to differentiate \(F(x) = \frac{1}{g(x)}\) so set \(f(u) = \frac{1}{u}\) and \(u=g(x)\text{.}\) Then the chain rule tells us

\begin{align*} \dfrac{\dd }{\dd x} \left\{\frac{1}{g(x)}\right\} = \dfrac{\dd F}{\dd x} \amp= \dfrac{\dd f}{\dd u} \cdot \diff{u}{x}\\ \amp= \frac{-1}{u^2} \cdot g'(x)\\ \amp= -\frac{g'(x)}{g(x)^2}. \end{align*}

Once we know this, a quick application of the product rule will give us the quotient rule.

\begin{align*} \amp\dfrac{\dd }{\dd x} \left\{ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right\} = \dfrac{\dd }{\dd x} \left\{ f(x) \cdot \frac{1}{g(x)} \right\} \amp \text{use PR}\\ \amp= f'(x)\cdot \frac{1}{g(x)} + f(x) \cdot \dfrac{\dd }{\dd x} \left\{\frac{1}{g(x)}\right\} \amp \text{use the result from above}\\ \amp= f'(x)\cdot \frac{1}{g(x)} - f(x) \cdot \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)^2} \amp \text{place over a common denominator}\\ \amp= \frac{f'(x) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot g'(x)}{g(x)^2} \end{align*}

which is exactly the quotient rule.
