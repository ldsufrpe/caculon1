Nota de rodapé 2.2.
For \(c\) to be a local maximum or minimum of \(f\text{,}\) the function \(f\) must obviously be defined at \(c\text{.}\) So here we are considering only points \(c\) in the domain of \(f\text{.}\) We will later, in §[cross-reference to target(s) "ssec_sketch_FD" missing or not unique], extend the definition of singular points of \(f\) to points that are not in the domain of \(f\text{.}\)
