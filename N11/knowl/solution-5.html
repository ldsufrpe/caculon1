Solução 1.12.1.

Podemos calcular isso usando a regra de L'Hôpital duas vezes:

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\rightarrow 0+}\underbrace{\frac{\log x} {\tan\big(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\big)}}_{\atp {\mathrm{num}\rightarrow -\infty} {\mathrm{den}\rightarrow +\infty}} \amp =\lim_{x\rightarrow 0+}\frac{\frac{1}{x}} {-\sec^2(\frac{\pi}{2}-x)} =-\lim_{x\rightarrow 0+}\underbrace{\frac{\cos^2(\frac{\pi}{2}-x)}{x}}_{\atp {\mathrm{num}\rightarrow 0} {\mathrm{den}\rightarrow 0}}\\ \amp=-\lim_{x\rightarrow 0+}\underbrace{\frac {2\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}-x)\sin(\frac{\pi}{2}-x)}{1}}_{\atp {\mathrm{num}\rightarrow 0} {\mathrm{den}\rightarrow 1}} =0 \end{align*}

A primeira aplicação do L'Hôpital foi com

\begin{align*} f(x) \amp= \log x \amp g(x) \amp=\tan\Big(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\Big)\\ f'(x)\amp=\frac{1}{x}\qquad\amp g'(x)\amp=-\sec^2\Big(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\Big) \end{align*}

e a segunda vez com

\begin{align*} f(x) \amp= \cos^2\Big(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\Big) \amp g(x) \amp=x\\ f'(x)\amp=2\cos\Big(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\Big) \Big[-\sin\Big(\frac{\pi}{2}-x\Big)\Big](-1)\qquad\amp g'(x)\amp=1 \end{align*}
