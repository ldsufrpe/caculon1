Differentiating \(\ell\) gives

\begin{align*} \dfrac{\dd \ell}{\dd \theta} \amp= -\frac{3 \cos\theta}{\sin^2\theta} + \frac{2\sin\theta}{\cos^2\theta} = \frac{-3\cos^3\theta +2 \sin^3\theta}{\sin^2\theta \cos^2\theta}. \end{align*}

This does not exist at \(\theta = 0, \frac{\pi}{2}\) (which we have already analysed) but does exist at every \(0 \lt \theta \lt \frac{\pi}{2}\) and is equal to zero when the numerator is zero. Namely when

\begin{align*} 2\sin^3\theta \amp= 3 \cos^3 \theta \amp \text{divide by $\cos^3 \theta$}\\ 2 \tan^3\theta \amp= 3\\ \tan\theta \amp= \sqrt[3]{\frac{3}{2}} \end{align*}
