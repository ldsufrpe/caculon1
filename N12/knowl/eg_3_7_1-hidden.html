Let \(q \gt 1\) and compute the limit

\begin{gather*} \lim_{x \to0} \frac{q^x - 1}{x} \end{gather*}

This limit arose in our discussion of exponential functions in Section [cross-reference to target(s) "sec_exp_func" missing or not unique].

  • First check

    \begin{align*} \lim_{x \to 0} (q^x-1) \amp = 1-1 = 0\\ \lim_{x \to 0} x \amp = 0 \end{align*}
    so we have a \(\frac00\) indeterminate form.

  • Set \(f(x)= q^x-1\) and \(g(x)=x\text{,}\) then (maybe after a quick review of Section [cross-reference to target(s) "sec_exp_func" missing or not unique])

    \begin{align*} f'(x) \amp= \dfrac{\dd}{\dd x}\left(q^x-1\right) = q^x \cdot \log q \amp f'(0) \amp= \log q\\ g'(x) \amp= 1 \amp g'(0) \amp= 1 \end{align*}

  • And by l'Hôpital's rule  3 

    \begin{align*} \lim_{h \to0} \frac{q^h - 1}{h} \amp= \log q. \end{align*}
