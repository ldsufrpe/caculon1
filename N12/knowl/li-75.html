We can now eliminate the variable \(y\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \ell^2 \amp= (x-7)^2 + (y-5)^2\\ \amp= (x-7)^2 + (6-3x-5)^2 = (x-7)^2 + (1-3x)^2\\ \amp= x^2-14x+49 + 1-6x+9x^2 = 10x^2-20x+50\\ \amp= 10(x^2-2x+5)\\ \ell \amp= \sqrt{10} \cdot \sqrt{x^2-2x+5} \end{align*}
Notice that as \(x \to \pm \infty\) the distance \(\ell \to +\infty\text{.}\)
