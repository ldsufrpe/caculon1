It has hypotenuse \(\sqrt{ 3^{2/3} + 2^{2/3}}\text{,}\) and so

\begin{align*} \sin \theta \amp= \frac{3^{1/3}}{\sqrt{3^{2/3}+2^{2/3}}}\\ \cos \theta \amp= \frac{2^{1/3}}{\sqrt{3^{2/3}+2^{2/3}}} \end{align*}

Alternatively could use the identities:

\begin{align*} 1 + \tan^2\theta \amp= \sec^2\theta \amp 1+\cot^2\theta \amp= \csc^2\theta \end{align*}

to obtain expressions for \(1/\cos\theta\) and \(1/\sin\theta\text{.}\)
