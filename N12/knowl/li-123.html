Now we can apply Corollary Corolário 2.13. The endpoints of the domain give
\begin{align*} \ell^2(-2) \amp= (-2-1)^2 + 1 - (-2)^2/4 = 3^2+1-1 = 9\\ \ell^2(2) \amp= (2-1)^2 + 1 - 2^2/4 = 1+1-1 = 1 \end{align*}
The derivative is
\begin{align*} \dfrac{\dd }{\dd x} \ell^2 \amp= 2(x-1) - x/2 = \frac{3x}{2} - 2 \end{align*}
Thus there are no singular points, but there is a critical point at \(x = 4/3\text{.}\) The corresponding squared-distance is
\begin{align*} \ell^2(4/3) \amp= \left( \frac{4}{3}-1\right)^2 +1 - \frac{(4/3)^2}{4}\\ \amp= (1/3)^2 + 1 - (4/9) = 6/9 = 2/3. \end{align*}
