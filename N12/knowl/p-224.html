A careful application of the chain rule shows that the derivative is

\begin{align*} \dfrac{\dd \theta}{\dd x} \amp= \frac{1}{1+(\frac{p+h}{x})^2}\cdot \left(\frac{-(p+h)}{x^2}\right) - \frac{1}{1+(\frac{p}{x})^2}\cdot \left(\frac{-p}{x^2}\right)\\ \amp= \frac{-(p+h)}{x^2+(p+h)^2} + \frac{p}{x^2+p^2} \end{align*}

So a critical point occurs when

\begin{align*} \frac{(p+h)}{x^2+(p+h)^2} \amp= \frac{p}{x^2+p^2} \amp \text{cross multiply}\\ (p+h)(x^2+p^2) \amp= p(x^2+(p+h)^2) \amp \text{collect $x$ terms}\\ x^2 (p+h-p) \amp= p(p+h)^2 - p^2 (p+h) \amp \text{clean up}\\ h x^2 \amp= p(p+h)(p+h-p) = ph(p+h)\\ \amp\hskip1in \text{cancel common factors}\\ x^2 \amp= p(p+h)\\ x \amp= \pm\sqrt{p(p+h)} \approx \pm 64.9m \end{align*}
