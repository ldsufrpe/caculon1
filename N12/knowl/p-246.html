By assumption \(f'(x)\) and \(g'(x)\) exist, with \(g'(x)\) nonzero, in some open interval around \(a\text{,}\) except possibly at \(a\) itself. So we know that they exist, with \(g'(x)\ne 0\text{,}\) in some interval \((a,b]\) with \(b \gt a\text{.}\) Then the GMVT (Theorem [cross-reference to target(s) "thm_GMVT" missing or not unique]) tells us that for \(x\in (a,b]\)

\begin{align*} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{g(x)-g(a)} \amp= \frac{f'(c)}{g'(c)} \end{align*}

where \(c \in (a,x)\text{.}\) As we take the limit as \(x\to a\text{,}\) we also have that \(c\to a\text{,}\) and so

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\to a^+}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \amp= \lim_{x\to a^+} \frac{f'(c)}{g'(c)} = \lim_{c \to a^+} \frac{f'(c)}{g'(c)} \end{align*}

as required.
